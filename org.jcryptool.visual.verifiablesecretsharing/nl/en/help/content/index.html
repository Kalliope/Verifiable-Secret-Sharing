Verifiable Secret Sharing

This demonstration hast the intention to show how the Verifiable Secret Sharing plug-in works.

The Verifiable Secret Sharing plug-in can be started at the menu bar at the point Visualization or at the view Algorithms in the tab Visualization.

The plug-in is splitted into 3 parts: The header, the main area itself and further explanations.

The Header shows a short description of the plug-in

The main area contains the boxes to set the parameters and all other computations of the Verifiable Secret Sharing-algorithm.

At the bottom of the screen you can find the area explanations where additional informations for the current step are shown.

Setting the parameters

The parameters, which are necessary for the algorithm, are set in the box Parameters.

At first there will be chosen the number persons n, which are concerned to get a share of the secret. Furthermore you have to decide about a number persons t how many persons will be needed to reconstruct it. The number of persons t may not be bigger than persons n. Both numbers have to be at least 2, as far as Verifiable Secret Sharing doesn't make sense for less involved people than 2.

The next parameter to choose is the secret s. After that, the module p, which have to be a prime, is calculated automatically. If you don't like to use the proposed prime, you have the possibility to enter any other prime as long it is bigger than the secret s. The last parameter is the primitive root g mod p. In modular arithmetic, a branch of number theory, a primitive root modulo p is any number g with the property that any number coprime to p is congruent to a power of g modulo p. In other words, g is a generator of the multiplicative group of integers modulo p. The primitive root g mod p will be calculated automatically like p. As it is with p, you also can choose g yourself. Though to the quite complex calculation of a primitive root, it is highly recommended to work with the proposed number.

If all parameters are set correctly, it is possible to go on to the next step with a click on Determine coefficients.

Determining the coefficients for the polynomial and calculating the commitments

The coefficients for the polynomial are set in this box. They can be generated by JCrypTool or chosen by yourself. The coefficients have to be natural numbers smaller than the module p The first coefficient is fixed, and is the secret s which already has been chosen.

In this box you can also find the button Commit. The computation of the commitments is necessary for the verification of the shares afterwards. The commitments are shown in a seperate box after they are calculated.

The computation of the commitments is optional, as far as they are just needed to check the shares afterwards. They are not needed for the reconstruction of the secret. If you want to do the reconstruction without checking the shares, you don't need the commitments.

Go on to the next step for calculating the shares with a click on Calculate shares

Checking the shares and reconstruction

The last step is splitted up in 2 boxes: Shares and Reconstruction

The box Shares shows the shares and the shares mod p. For checking the shares, a click on the respective Check-button is necessary. Keep in mind that the check won't be succesful if you didn't have done the calculating of the commitments first. To show the sense of checking the shares it is possible to change one or more shares. The change of one or more shares has 2 consequences. At first the check for the modified share won't be successful anymore. And second the reconstruct won't return the primordial secret.

The reconstruction can be done in the box Reconstruction. Before you can start with a click on reconstruct it is essential to choose the shares you want to use. It is irrelevant which ones you take as long as equal or more than the minimum. The minimum is the number of persons t you have chosen in the beginning.
